> Practice isn't learning — it's improving what you already learned. Don't just
> loop practice. Make time to learn something new. Break the loop.

It's easy to mistake repetition for progress. You sit down, run through the things
your hands already know, and it feels productive. But repeating what you can
already do isn't the same as learning — it's polishing. Both matter, but only one
of them moves you forward.

So balance your time. Give space to a proper **warmup** so you actually sound good,
to **technique** so you can get around your instrument better, to **projects** that
improve specific pieces, and to **review** so you keep your repertoire alive and
have some fun while you're at it.

The point isn't to practice more. It's to make sure some of that time is spent
learning something you couldn't do yesterday.
